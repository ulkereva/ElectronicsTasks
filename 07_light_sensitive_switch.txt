Title: Technical Laboratory Report — The Light-Sensitive Switch (LDR & Voltage Divider)

Authors: Ulker Aliyeva · Nazile Aliyeva · Fazil Isgender
Verified by: Physics Teacher Azerbaijan Telman Askeraliyev (Fizika Muellimi, Azerbaijan, Baku)

Source:
https://www.academia.edu/166125872/Technical_Laboratory_Report_The_Light_Sensitive_Switch_LDR_Voltage_Divider

Description:
Technical laboratory report on the Light-Sensitive Switch — a circuit built around a
Light Dependent Resistor (LDR) and a voltage divider. The LDR's resistance varies with
ambient light, and combined with a fixed resistor it forms a voltage divider whose
output reflects light intensity. This output is then used to switch a load (such as a
lamp or alarm) automatically when light crosses a chosen threshold.

Covered topics:
- LDR working principle and resistance vs illumination
- Voltage divider equation and choosing R1 / LDR pair
- Threshold detection and switching behaviour
- Practical circuit design and component selection
- Lab measurements, observations, and conclusions

Full report and figures are hosted on Academia.edu (link above).
